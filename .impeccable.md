## Design Context

### Users
Solo user (the app owner) drilling Japanese on their phone during commutes. Mobile-first, one-handed, possibly distracted, short bursts. No multi-user or teacher/classroom scenarios.

### Brand Personality
Quiet, studious, textbook-like. Calm and focused, not gamified. Voice is plain and factual — no exclamation points, no streak confetti, no "Nice job!" chirp. Think of a Kodansha reference book more than a Duolingo owl.

### Aesthetic Direction
Understated. Serious. Print-inspired. Japanese vocabulary is the hero — the prompt (kana/kanji) should feel like a specimen on a page, surrounded by quiet whitespace. No gradients, no glow, no neon. Neutral palette with restrained accent; typography carries the weight. Reasonable on both light and dark depending on commute lighting.

### Design Principles
1. The prompt is sacred — nothing competes with the Japanese character in the center of the screen.
2. Mobile-first, one-thumb reachable. Bottom-heavy action placement over top/center for primary input.
3. Show only what's needed for the current phase (setup / drill / review). No simultaneous sidebars, stats walls, or metric dashboards.
4. Legibility over decoration. Type hierarchy and spacing do the work; color is for answer feedback only.
5. No gamification tells: no streaks, no fireworks, no sound-effect energy. Feedback is a quiet "correct / incorrect" and the correct answer.
